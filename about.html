About the styles

Ballet and lyrical are considered to be the "pretty" styles. It is almost always slow songs that you dance to. Ballet is the core of dance. That is where all of your technique comes from. The more ballet classes you take the more your technique will grow. Lyrical is not as technical but you usually dance to a slower song as well. A lot of times during these styles you tell a story through your movements. Most times they can be a little upsetting and you have to show that through your facial expressions. The best is when you are able to relate to a story you are portraying. The emotion will just flow out naturally.

Vocabulary

  • Plie- to bend
  • Releve- to rise
  • Tendu- to strench
  • Glissade- to glide
  • Saute- to jump
  • Elance- to dart
  • Tourne- to turn

Why I never liked Ballet and only Lyrical...

Ballet was never my favorite style. I was not a big fan of the slow music and everything you had to do be so precise. I was always into the fast pace styles. I did not start lyrical until I got a little older. I fell in love with that style right away. It blew my mind because I thought that would just go right along with ballet on my list that I did not like. Yes lyrical is slow but not as slow as ballet. There was no way of getting out of ballet either since it was a requirement for me to take. After years went by and I changed dance studios I started to like ballet. Then it hit me...I only disliked ballet because of the instructor that taught it. I started loving to go to ballet class because of my new instructor.
